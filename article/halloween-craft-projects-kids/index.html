Halloween Craft Projects for Kids

  1. Mariam Bonaparte
  2. October 5, 2012 9:08 pm

Every year people, be it adults, adolescents or kids look forward to Halloween. There’s no end to the planning that goes on till everyone is all set and ready for Halloween. For kids mothers can help them get their homes all done up for the occasion and in the bargain rope in the kids to help with various themed ventures that they can make. Now when it concerns kids the themes need not necessarily have to be frightening, they can be assisted to create charming ghost-like lollipops, puppets form paper bags or funny looking pumpkin plates. This can be made a joint venture with all the moms in the block or area getting together to chart out a crafts evening.

Enjoyable Halloween Handicrafts
This is one time, during Halloween, that parents especially the moms get to having some precious time with their kids creating handicrafts for Halloween, decorating their homes with something entirely diverse topic when compared to what they would be doing through the other times of the year. Halloween spells ghosts and scary themes giving the kids a chance to let their imagination and their innovative skills to run wild.

The great thing is that making crafts for Halloween need not be a pricey affair. These can be done with things that you have in the house, like paper bags, old bags that were used to buy grocery and the like.

Making a ghost out of a grocery bag is interesting and easy. You will need a full blown balloon preferably with helium so that it will stay afloat close to the ceiling without having to tie it down. The helium will ensure that the balloon will float about and not be tethered to one place. If the balloon is white, it is better, to make the ghost more authentic.

You will then require some white grocery plastic bags form which the handles would have to be snipped off. Now cut the bag into a rectangular shape and cut strips from it about a finger in width. Stick these strips on the balloon with paste or tape. As you get to the bottom of the balloon let the strips hang down making them appear like octopus tentacles. With that done, use glow markers to draw the ghost’s mouth and eyes. When are hung just low enough to brush the heads and shoulders of the guests, it quite definitely would give them an eerie feeling.

Edible jewelry
There are ever so many ideas that can keep the brats engaged. Teach the kids to pass a blunt threaded needle through candied corn or for that matter even marshmallows or something soft and eatable. These can be made into necklaces or bracelets. Ensure that the ones worn round the neck are large enough to pass comfortably over their heads. Another idea is to create ghost lollipops by covering the lollipops with white paper. Get the kid to draw the face of a ghost on it. The paper can be tied at the bottom of the candy with colored ribbons that curl, or groovy looking strings.

Decorate the porch
One of the most excellent ways to do up the front of your home is with a pumpkin carved into a lantern, but with kids who may not be up to the carving of a pumpkin, they can be assisted to paint a frightening or stupid looking face in its place. In addition they could also be helped to paint or draw on paper bags that can be blown out, tied and hung along the verandah. It would look even better with a small electric light placed inside them thereby showing up the kids’ handiwork.

Crafts with Paper Plates
For a children’s Halloween party, each kid can be offered a paper plate that they have to do up with marker pens. Help the kids and given them ideas about how to go about decorating their respective plates. Once they have completed the decorating part, place each plate with the decorated part facing down, fill the plates with some sort of seeds, tiny pebbles or the like, , place another plate above it and stick or staple the two together, ensuring that there is no gap for the seeds or pebbles to fall out. Kids would have fun making noises with their plates through the night.

Spiders from pipe cleaners
You can use a variety of things that you find around the house to engage kids in Halloween crafts. One such item is the pipe cleaner that can be converted to look like spiders. Assist the kids to make eight pieces from four pipe cleaners. Now you will also need a foam ball. The kids will also require paint to decorate the ball. By sticking four pieces of pipe cleaner to one side of the ball and the remaining four pieces into the y. opposite side the kid would have a creepy spider all done and ready. Bigger spiders can be created using a bigger ball or old milk jug or a juice can that is no longer needed. Remember the child would have to paint the jug or can with black paint.

Spiders can also be made from recycled cartons of eggs, wherein you just cut out the cups, paint them black and stick pipe cleaners, four on each side to make the legs. To make it look more realistic just give the pipe cleaners a slight bend. If this is hung from the ceiling on some sort of material that has a slight spring to it, like a long piece of elastic or the like the spider would bob up and down in the dimness of the room and with the fan turning.

Bags for Tricks or treats
For this you can utilize old and resurrected pillowcases. Give a pillowcase to each child to do up as he pleases with marker pens or paints that can be used on fabric. Help the child attach sequins or some sort of gilt powder to make it look shiny and glittering.

These are only some ideas, if you are one with a creative bent of mind, then surely you can concoct far more crafty ideas for the kids Halloween party.
